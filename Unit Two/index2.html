May Olvera, Nov. 15, 2019
Borough Park hit with string of anti-Semitic attacks

Private security cameras all across Borough Park tell stories of violence and hatred endured by its Jewish community.

In recent weeks, Borough Park has seen a spike in hate crimes committed against its Jewish citizens. Two arrests have been made in connection to anti-semitic aggressions in the neighborhood but antisemitic violence has yet to calm down.

“We’ve sent a very strong message to the police department that there needs to be some serious proactivity on this matter,” said Avi Greenstein, CEO of the Boro Park Jewish Community Council, a social service non-profit that assists Jews and non-Jews alike.

On Nov. 9, Greenstein and members of the Boro Park Shomrim—a volunteer-based neighborhood policing organization—reviewed security footage from cameras outside of the Jewish Community Council’s headquarters. Two women were harassed with anti-semitic slurs the previous evening as they were leaving the building.

“The police department has increased their presence but the Shomrim are the ones spending time with me on a voluntary basis at 12 o’clock on a Saturday night looking at surveillance footage,” he said. “Not the police department.”

Greenstein said many hate crimes go unreported due to a lack of resolve. NYPD’s annual hate crime reports are consistent with his observation.



For the full year of 2018, 353 hate crime complaints were filed. Of those complaints, the most common bias motivation identified by NYPD were 186 anti-Jewish incidents, more than half of the total reported.

However, NYPD only made 150 hate crime arrests that year, 69 of which were anti-Jewish. In the 66th police precinct, which includes Borough Park, there were 14 complaints filed for anti-Jewish hate crimes but only seven arrests made.

Currently, NYPD has only released bias-specific hate crime statistics for the first three quarters of 2019. The first three quarters of the year saw 309 reported incidents across the city, 166 anti-Jewish. This is an increase from the first three quarters of 2018, which had 250 hate crimes, 108 anti-Jewish.

In Brooklyn’s precincts specifically, reports of anti-Jewish hate crimes increased from 58 in 2017 to 77 in 2018 and 82 in the first 3 quarters of 2019.
